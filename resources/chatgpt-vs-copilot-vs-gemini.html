ChatGPT vs Copilot vs Gemini vs Claude: which should your business use?

By Dylan Bailey, Certified Claude Expert
Updated 15 July 2026 · 7 min read
The short answer

There’s no single winner. For most Australian businesses the best AI tool comes down to two things: the software you already use, and the work you do. Microsoft 365 shop → Copilot. Google Workspace → Gemini. Want a flexible standalone assistant for writing and research → ChatGPT or Claude, both excellent. The tools matter less than whether your staff know how to use them — and the skill transfers between all four.

“Which AI should we use?” is one of the most common questions we get. The honest answer frustrates people who want a single name — but it’s also freeing, because it means you can’t really pick wrong. Here’s how the four main options compare for a business, and how to choose.

The honest answer: it depends on your existing tools

All four of these tools are genuinely capable. For everyday business tasks — drafting, summarising, analysing, brainstorming — you’d be well served by any of them. So the deciding factor usually isn’t raw ability; it’s fit with the software your team already lives in. An AI that works inside the apps you use all day removes friction, and friction is what stops staff actually adopting it.

Match the tool to how your team already works

  • You run on Microsoft 365 — Copilot is the path of least resistance. It sits inside Word, Excel, Outlook and Teams, so it can help with the documents and emails your team is already in.
  • You run on Google Workspace — Gemini is the natural match, built into Gmail, Docs and Sheets.
  • You want a powerful, flexible assistant that isn’t tied to a suite — ChatGPT and Claude are both outstanding. Claude has a reputation for careful, natural writing and working through longer documents; ChatGPT for breadth and its large feature set. Trying both for a week costs nothing and settles most debates.

If you mainly want a customer-facing chatbot

“Best AI for business” searches often really mean “a chatbot to answer customer questions on our website.” That’s a slightly different job. The tools above are assistants your staff use; a customer-facing chatbot is usually a dedicated product (often built on top of one of these models) that you train on your own information. If that’s your goal, start by writing down the exact questions you want it to handle — the quality of that groundwork matters more than the brand of AI underneath.

The skill that outlasts the tool choice

Here’s the part most comparisons miss: whichever tool you pick, the thing that determines your results is whether your people can use it well. A skilled user gets more out of a “second-best” tool than an untrained one gets out of the “best”. And because the core skills — prompting well, knowing which tasks to trust to AI, building agents and automations — are the same across all four, you’re never locked in. Train the skill, and switching tools later is trivial.

Frequently asked questions

Which AI tool is best for business?
There’s no single best tool — it mostly depends on the software you already use and the work you do. If you run on Microsoft 365, Copilot fits most naturally; on Google Workspace, Gemini does. ChatGPT and Claude are both strong general-purpose choices for writing, analysis and research. For most businesses the bigger win is training staff to use whichever tool well, because the skills transfer between them.
Is Microsoft Copilot the same as ChatGPT?
They’re related but not the same. Copilot is Microsoft’s assistant built into apps like Word, Excel, Outlook and Teams, and it has used OpenAI’s models under the hood. ChatGPT is OpenAI’s own standalone product. The main practical difference is integration: Copilot works inside your Microsoft documents and data, while ChatGPT is a separate chat tool you go to directly.
Should a small business use ChatGPT or Gemini?
If your business lives in Google Workspace (Gmail, Docs, Sheets), Gemini is the natural fit because it works inside those apps. If you mostly want a flexible, standalone AI assistant for writing, research and general tasks, ChatGPT is an excellent all-rounder. Many small businesses happily use one of each — the important part is that staff know how to prompt well.
What is Claude best at compared to ChatGPT?
Claude, made by Anthropic, is widely regarded as strong at longer-form writing, careful reasoning over documents, and following instructions closely with a natural, measured tone — with a focus on safety. ChatGPT is an extremely capable all-rounder with a large ecosystem of features. In practice both are excellent; the right choice depends on your tasks and preferences more than any single scoreboard.
What if I pick the wrong AI tool?
It’s a low-risk decision. Because the underlying skill — writing clear prompts and knowing which tasks to hand to AI — transfers between tools, staff who learn one can move to another quickly. Most tools also offer free tiers, so you can trial before committing. Focus on building the skill, not agonising over the tool.
